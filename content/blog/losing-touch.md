Ever felt yourself changing at a fundamental level, and slowly becoming a different person? Slowly... being unable to recognize yourself, becoming a stranger to your own mind?

I hear adolescence is like that. But, maybe because I was so sheltered, it's only taking effect now.

I don't code anymore... at least, not as much as I used to. I don't feel motivated to. It feels like I lost a core part of myself. Coding was the fibre of my being. Without code, who am I? It's something I'm having to come to terms with these days.

It's good to diversify, not just in investing, but also in interests. Though, it didn't feel like it was in my control. Events in my life naturally led me to feel connected with coding; I couldn't just artificially grow feelings for new hobbies; at least not to the level of my affinity with coding.

What is in my control, though, is "to love it enough to let it go". Nothing in life lasts forever; it's sad to see it pass me by, but I'm glad I ever did cross paths with it. Best I can do is keep experiencing life as it comes to me, and exploring my abilities however opportunity presents.

Ofc, I will keep coding, when it comes to me naturally. But gone are the days of losing myself in projects, building, solving problems in my life with code. As life went on, my problems diminished (Alhamdulillah), but that did lead to some level of monotone. I do have some ideas of what I want to do next tho. Hopefully I'll find new love in something, or someone...
